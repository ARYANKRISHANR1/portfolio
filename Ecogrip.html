EcoGrip

Overview

With the global surge in smartphone usage, plastic phone covers have become a significant environmental hazard. EcoGrip was developed as an entrepreneurship project to solve this "plastic curse." It is a 100% compostable smartphone case designed to be buried in the ground at the end of its life cycle. Instead of contributing to landfill waste, the case breaks down into nutrient-rich manure, while an embedded seed germinates to grow into a new plant.

My Role & What I Did

  • Product Conceptualization: Identified the environmental gap in the mobile accessory market and co-developed the "Life-to-Plant" product cycle.
  • Material Science & R&D: Selected and tested a sustainable composite material using PHA (Polyhydroxyalkanoates), coconut fiber, and wood flour to ensure the case was both durable for daily use and rapidly biodegradable.
  • Prototyping: Integrated plant seeds into the material structure without compromising the case's integrity or the seed's ability to grow once buried.
  • Entrepreneurial Strategy: Developed the business case for a green alternative in a high-growth consumer electronics market.

The Outcome

The project was a major academic and functional success, receiving a near-perfect score of 98/100. We successfully proved that high-performance tech accessories can be created without synthetic plastics. The prototype effectively demonstrated the circular economy model: protecting a device during its life and nourishing the earth after its use.
