Royal Enfield UX+

Overview

While Royal Enfield’s official website is rich in heritage and culture, the lack of clear segmentation can be overwhelming for new riders. To prevent potential customers from leaving the site due to choice paralysis, I developed a prototype for a categorized add-on page. This tool simplifies the selection process by guiding users through a logical flow based on their specific needs, effectively acting as a digital "bike consultant."

My Role & What I Did

  • UX Gap Analysis: Identified that the current website architecture lacked a streamlined path for users to filter motorcycles by practical attributes, potentially leading to high bounce rates.
  • Information Architecture (IA): Designed a comprehensive decision tree that maps bikes based on three primary pillars: Engine Capacity, Utility/Riding Style, and Price Range.
  • High-Fidelity Prototyping: Built a website prototype that mirrors the official Royal Enfield aesthetic while integrating a new "Find Best Bike For You" feature.
  • Feature Development: Created interactive filters—such as "Relaxed Highway Cruising" vs. "Dynamic City Riding"—to translate technical specs into relatable user benefits.

The Outcome

I successfully created a functional, high-fidelity UI/UX prototype that provides a more intuitive browsing experience. By adding a clear "Start" path and simplified categories, the project demonstrates how to reduce user friction and increase the likelihood of a test ride booking. The final output includes a clear mapping of the entire 350cc to 650cc lineup, ensuring every user find a bike that fits their lifestyle in just a few clicks.
