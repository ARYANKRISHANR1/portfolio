Optimizing Social Media for Brand Growth

Using Neuromarketing Visual Saliency

Overview

This project explores a live, real-world application of neuromarketing visual saliency principles to optimize organic social media content strategy on Instagram. Conducted as a 3-credit MBA project under the mentorship of Dr. Anuj Kapoor at IIT Jodhpur, the study analyzes a 90-day field experiment on the personal brand account @surpassing69. The experiment challenged standard algorithmic growth assumptions by testing how pre-conscious human attention mechanics (such as chromatic contrast, facial salience, schema recognition, and emotional arousal) drive engagement independently of pre-existing follower counts or paid promotion budgets.

My Role & What I Did

  • Experimental Design & Field Execution: Formulated and launched a multi-phase live content strategy over a 90-day tracking window, actively publishing 73 reels to capture clean primary behavioral dataset insights.
  • Neuromarketing Asset Engineering: Designed and systematically deployed a high-contrast branded thumbnail template utilizing a distinct red-burst chromatic format, directly testing the "red-urgency effect" on bottom-up user attention.
  • Variables Tracking & Analytics Auditing: Isolated key independent design attributes (e.g., face presence ratios, text trigger triggers like 'FIGHT') and correlated them against native algorithmic metrics (views, shares, saves) using third-party verification via SocialStats.io.
  • Framework Construction: Synthesized field findings with cognitive behavioral literature (including Kahneman’s System 1/System 2 fluency effects and the Zeigarnik effect) to produce an actionable 3-layer optimization framework (Reach, Viral, and Depth layers) scalable for bootstrapped digital brands.

The Outcome

The experiment yielded conclusive evidence supporting the primary neuro-centric growth hypotheses. Reels leveraging engineered, high-contrast branded thumbnails achieved an explicit 4.4x view count advantage relative to unbranded baselines. Furthermore, high-arousal emotional visual triggers generated a massive breakout share metric (up to 132 organic shares on top-performing content), while direct face-dominant layouts successfully maximized pro-social engagement depth (securing a dataset peak 5.77% like-to-view rate). The ultimate 20.26% overall engagement rate demonstrated that sub-150ms visual layout optimization serves as a primary organic traction driver in hyper-competitive social feed architectures.
