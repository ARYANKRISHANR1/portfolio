Bombay Shaving Company: Shaving Off the Old FMCG Playbook

Overview

This project analyzes how Bombay Shaving Company (BSC) utilized a D2C-first DNA to challenge legacy giants like Gillette in India's rapidly growing men's grooming market. The core of the project was solving a "Conversion Paradox"—where high traffic (85% online mix) was only resulting in a 1.3% conversion rate, well below the 2% industry benchmark. The strategy focused on transitioning from simple "routine" messaging to emotional "ritual" storytelling.

Strategic Frameworks Applied

SWOT Analysis

  • Strengths: D2C agility, deep consumer data, and premium design aesthetic.
  • Weaknesses: Limited offline reach and low initial trust due to price premiums.
  • Opportunities: Expansion into gifting, sachet innovation for trials, and sustainable packaging.
  • Threats: Intense rivalry from global entrants and aggressive influencer-led brands.

Porter’s Five Forces

  • Rivalry: Intense; differentiation via brand narrative is critical.
  • Buyer Power: Moderate; urban men are convenience-led but price-insensitive for premium quality.
  • Supplier Power: Low; driven by a contract manufacturing model.

The Growth Funnel (Acquire, Convert, Retain, Advocate)

  • Used Dynamic Creative Optimization to increase CTR by 40%.
  • Applied UX Simplification to target a 2.5% Conversion Rate.
  • Implemented AI CRM for behavioral replenishment to boost repeat purchases by 30%.

My Role & What I Did

  • Data-Driven Product Ideation: Utilized social listening and Google Trends query mining to identify market demand for new segments, such as charcoal-based products.
  • UX & Tech Optimization: Directed a tech stack overhaul that reduced site load time from 11s to 6s and moved key UI elements to "right-thumb zones" for mobile-first users.
  • A/B Testing & Behavioral Design: Conducted split testing which proved that lifestyle-based visuals outperformed product-only imagery by 40%.
  • Retention Architecture: Designed a 10-step post-purchase CRM email flow and gamified referral programs to increase Customer Lifetime Value.

The Outcome

The strategic interventions led to significant improvements across all key performance indicators (KPIs):

  • CPC Reduction: Lowered Cost Per Click from ₹21 to ₹13 (a 38% reduction).
  • Engagement Boost: Achieved a 1.0% CTR, representing a 54% increase in engagement.
  • Bounce Rate: Realized a 20% reduction in site bounce rate through UX improvements.
  • Retention: Drove a 30% increase in repeat purchase rates through automated replenishment nudges.

Strategic Roadmap for the Future

  • Short Term (0-6 Months): Focus on "Funnel Repair" through site speed and sachet-led trials to widen the customer base.
  • Mid Term (6-12 Months): Launch the "BSC Tribe" loyalty program and expand into men’s fragrance and intimate hygiene.
  • Long Term (12+ Months): International expansion via Amazon Global and positioning the brand as a "Modern Masculinity" movement.
